PREVENTIVE MEASURES TO SAVE RIVERS
    A lot of efforts need to be taken in order to clean the river water and make it portable for drinking as it was before. Both Government and citizens needs to work in unison in order to get the work done. Rivers are a great treasure of our nation and we all definitely need to take necessary steps to safeguard it and for well being of all.

    In order to clean the rivers, the Government has started many cleanliness drives. 'Namami Gange' is one among them. It is one of the biggest initiative taken by Government in order to clean River Ganga. It was started in June 2014 with a budget of 20,000 crore rupees with an aim to clean, afforest and rejenuvate river Ganga. Its' main objectives include :
1) Afforestation
2) Creating awareness
3) Industrial Effluent Monitoring
4) Bio-Diversity
5) Sewerage Treatment Infrastructure
6) River-Front Development
7) River-Surface Cleaning
8) Ganga Gram
    Countries such as Australia, United Kingdom, Germany, Finland, Israel etc. have shown interest in collaborating with India for Ganga rejuvenation.

    Cleanliness Drive was even organised at the Ghats of Yamuna River by Delhi Government with an aim to rejenuvate River Yamuna. Many people took active participation in the programme and extended their support towards cleaning River Yamuna.

    As a responsible citizen even we need to contribute on our part. It can be done in many ways.


>

    Inspite of immersing the flowers, coconut and all the other ritualistic materials in river water, we must keep them in pots kept near temples or rivers. From these pots, these things are taken and are used for creating fertilizers, manure, etc. Hence it can be re-used thereby helping reduce the pollution. Adopting this technique can help reduce major amount of garbage from rivers.

    People must be given education regarding the importance of rivers so that they can stop carrying their morning rituals, washing clothes, taking their animals to bath near river water which also accounts for major part of pollution. Awareness campaigns must be organised and strict actons must be taken regarding people breaking the rules. Use of plastic must be completely banned and instead cloth bags must be used to stop collection of garbage at one place and avoid water blocking.

    Cleanliness Dives must be organised atleast once a week and people must be encouraged to actively participate in such drives. Just as said "Rome was not built in a day" similarly we cannot change the current scenario of rivers in a day. We definitely need to join hands and come together because together we can make a change and gradually help our rivers get back to what it must be-clean, pollution-free and safe for everyone.
