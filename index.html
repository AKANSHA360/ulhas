ULHAS RIVER

    The Ulhas river located in Western India is west flowing river located in the state of Maharashtra which drains an area of 4,637 sq.km.

    The Ulhas River originates in a valley located to the north of Rajmachi hills formed by mountain streams.The northern slopes of these hills which are a part of Sahyadri Ranges of Western Ghats is drained by this river. It then enters Thane and Raigad districts of Maharashtra between Kulgaon-Badlapur towards north of Ulhasnagar and then moves towards Kalyan. Then the river turns in west direction towards Thane and gets connected in to Thane creek.

    During its course it discharges water of Barvi Dam. At Thane , it seperates into two, of which one branch flows in west direction while the other flows in south direction.

    The river then turns in North-West direction towards Godbunder, where it opens into an estuary known as Vasai Creek. The Thane creek empties into Bombay harbour.

    The Ulhas River is a source of drinking water for the cities of Badlapur, Navi Mumbai, Kalyan, Dombivli.
